---

Imagine storing customer information for a business:

Without a database (variables in memory):
- Write customer data on sticky notes
- Keep them on your desk
- When you close the office (restart server), all notes are thrown away
- No way to search through thousands of notes efficiently
- Notes get lost, damaged, or mixed up

With a database (persistent storage):
- Organized filing cabinet with labeled drawers
- Customer files stay there even when office closes
- Can quickly find any customer using the index
- Multiple people can access files simultaneously
- Backup copies exist for safety

Databases are like permanent, organized filing systems for your application's data. They persist information even when your server restarts!
