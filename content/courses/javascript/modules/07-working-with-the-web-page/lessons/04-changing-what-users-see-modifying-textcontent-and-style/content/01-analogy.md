title: "The Smart Billboard"
---

Imagine you are in charge of a massive digital billboard in Times Square.

1.  **Changing the Message (`textContent`):** You have a remote control that lets you change the words on the screen. One second it says "Good Morning," and the next it says "Sale Starts Now!" The billboard structure doesn't change; only the **information** inside it does.
2.  **Changing the Appearance (`style`):** You can also change the background color from black to neon pink, or make the font twice as large. You are changing the **visual settings** of the billboard.
3.  **The Wardrobe Change (`classList`):** Instead of manually changing every color and font, you have "presets." You press a button for "Night Mode," and the billboard automatically applies a whole set of styles (dark background, white text, dim brightness). 

In JavaScript, we use these same tools to update our webpages. We can swap text, tweak colors, or swap entire "themes" by adding and removing CSS classes.
